What is Recycling?

Recycling:
process of turning materials into something new instead of
throwing it away

Why Should I Recycle?

Recycling and Solid Waste Division collects approximately a total of 250,000 tons of waste annually from Sacramento, while only 35,000 tons of material is recycled a year. The EPA approximated that 75% of waste in the U.S. is recyclable, but only 30% of the waste stream is actually recycled. In other words, recyclable waste is piling up in landfills when the items could potentially be turned into something new instead.

How Do I start?

  1. Clean products and ensure that there is no food waste
    • Why? To prevent cross-contamination
  2. Put items into the blue bin, which is also known as the
    mixed recyclable bin

What Can Be Recycled

PLASTIC

  • CRV Plastic
  • Containers 1 - 7
  • Soda Liter Bottles
  • Water Bottles
    • remember to separate the caps from the bottle
  • Milk jugs
  • Shampoo bottles

PAPER

  • Cardboard
    • must be flattened before putting in the blue bin
  • Cereal boxes
  • Newspaper
  • Magazines
  • Paper bags
  • Paper packaging
  • Junk mail
  • Phone books
  • Office paper (accepted in all colors)

What Can NOT Be Recycled

PLASTIC

  • Styrofoam Containers
  • Plastic bags
  • Plastic Utensils

PAPER

  • Shredded Paper
  • Binders
  • Used Paper Products (like tissues)
  • Waxed paper
  • Foil-lined paper

MISC.

  • Caprisuns
  • Anything made of mixed materials
  • Gloves
  • Masks
